NGOZI OKONJO-IWEALA

First Nigerian woman to hold the position of Finance Minister for two terms.

Ngozi Okonjo Iweala, one woman I highly adore. She is the World Trade Organization's first female and first African director-general, She is also a Nigerian economist who took office on March 3, 2021. (WTO). She oversaw efforts at the World Bank that gave low-income nations' economies priority and was also the finance minister of Nigeria for the longest period of time. Ngozi Okonjo-Iweala was born in Ogwashi-Ukwu, Delta State, Nigeria, on June 13, 1954. When she was a teenager, she went to the United States to study at Harvard University after attending the oldest girls' secondary school in Nigeria. Here's one of her quotes that I particularly like:


"Africa is a continent of many countries, not one country.
If we are down to three or four conflicts,
it means that there are plenty of opportunities to invest in stable,
growing, exciting economies where there's plenty of opportunity."

    NGOZI OKONJO-IWEALA'S TIMELINE
    • Ngozi Okonjo-Iweala was born in Ogwashi-Uku, Delta State, Nigeria, on June 13, 1954.
    • She earned an A.B. in economics from Harvard University in 1976, a Master's in City Planning from the Massachusetts Institute of Technology in 1978, and a Ph.D. in regional economics and development from the Massachusetts Institute of Technology in 1981.
    • She worked at the World Bank for two terms, the first as a development economist and vice president and corporate secretary of the World Bank Group (1982–2003) and later as managing director of operations (2007–11).
    • During the global financial crisis of 2007–08 and the global food-price crisis of 2008–09, she coordinated various World Bank programmes to assist disadvantaged countries.
    • Dr. Okonjo-Iweala served as Nigeria's Finance Minister twice (from 2003 to 2006 and again from 2011 to 2015), as well as briefly as Foreign Minister in 2006, making her the first woman to hold both positions.
    • She is known for being the first female and African candidate to run for the World Bank Group presidency in 2012, with support from Africa and key developing countries in the first truly competitive election for the world's highest development finance office.
    • She was one of the top 100 most powerful women in the world from 2011 to 2014, as well as one of the top three most powerful women in Africa (2012).
    • Nigeria's This Day newspaper named her Minister of the Decade, People's Choice Award (2020), one of Transparency International's 8 Female Anti-Corruption Fighters Who Inspire (2019), and one of the 50 Greatest World Leaders (2019). (Fortune, 2015).
    • She has over 30 years of experience working in Asia, Africa, Europe, Latin America, and North America as a global financial specialist, economist, and international development professional.
    • She is the seventh Director-General of the World Trade Organization.
